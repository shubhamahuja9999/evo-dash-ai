The game isn't over once the demo is booked, or trial is had.

"You'll come to realize that 85% of ad creatives fail. Your ability to find winners FAST is key to your success, this is where frameworks help 👇🏻


After obsessing over creative testing methods over the years, I've distilled it down into a simple 3-part framework that ANYONE can use. 


We use it across our $3M/month ad spend portfolio of SaaS clients. 


Don't underestimate the power of it's simplicity, here it is: 


Part 1: Concept 
Part 2: Copy 
Part 3: Color 


This is the order of operations to iterate and find winners FASTER. 


Let's start with the concept, then copy, and finally color. 


1/ Concept 


This is all around testing UNIQUE creative concepts. 


Concepts are the overall, look, feel, and style of your ads. In a world of B2B where we're testing 1-2 lines of copy, we've found the concept to dive the most significant lift in CTR compared to other elements. Simply put you want to test RADICALLY different concepts, for example: memes vs product mockups vs before/afters Testing creative concepts first requires more design resources but it will result in the user seeing different variations of ads rather than small iterations of a single concept. 


2/ Copy 


Once you've found a winning concept it's time to test copy variations.


This includes the creative headline, intro text, and call-to-action. You can also test copy length here (don't be afraid of long form! - especially for cold audiences). Copy testing should tie to a messaging story so that you're getting valuable insights on not just what ad drives the most clicks but what messaging resonates with your audience so you can take those learnings and apply them across your marketing efforts. 


3/ Color 


This last phase is circumstantial depending on type of ad you're testing.


If you're testing video ads this obviously would not apply. Only here at the end does it make sense to iterate color tests on a winning concept and messaging variation. It can make a difference but it's usually marginal (despite what you hear in blogs). Typically we iterate in cycles off just the concept + copy variations 


Final thoughts: 


The goal is to find your winners faster, this framework will help you do that."

GoDaddy starts their YouTube ad with a logo animation for the first second.

Slow pages = low quality score. Make sure your landing page aligns with the ad’s keywords for better conversion rates.

Start with manual CPC if you have zero conversions. But once you hit 10+ per campaign, let Google’s algorithm do the work.


6️⃣ Limit RSAs per ad group


More ads ≠ better performance. Stick to one RSA per ad group (max two) to help Google find a winning combination faster.


7️⃣ Pin your headlines


Don’t leave it to chance. Use this formula:
• Headline 1: Target keyword
• Headline 2: Unique selling point or social proof

If 90% of your conversions happen on desktop, consider reducing mobile spend. Follow the data.

Competitors bidding on your name? You need a brand campaign. Run a holdout test in one region and measure the impact.

"Please STOP launching new ad campaigns.

If you already have active campaigns, do this instead:


• Take your total monthly budget and divide by 30.4
• Divide that number by $100 and $50
• Multiply that by 3


You're left with how many active campaigns and ads you can run.


For example, with a monthly budget of $50,000:


• $50,000 / 30.4 = $1,644 daily spend 
• $1,644/100 and 50 = 16-32 active campaigns
• 16-32 x 3 = 48-96 active ads


So this means with a daily budget of $50-100, 16-32 active campaigns.


Assuming a minimum of 3 ads per campaign that's 48-96 ad variations. 


This is a simple way to make sure you're not spreading your budget thin.
